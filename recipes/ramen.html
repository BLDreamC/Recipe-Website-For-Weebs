Naruto Ramen

Steps and Estimated Preparation Time

  1. Shoyu Tare (30 minutes)
  2. Pork Chashu (1 hour)
  3. Menma(Seasoned Bamboo Shoots) (30 minutes + Overnight)
  4. Seasoned Eggs (10 minutes + Overnight)
  5. Broth (4 hours + Overnight)
  6. Placing It All Together
    Ingredients
      1 Package of Ramen
      2 Cups of Pork or Beef Broth (or make own with pork bones)
      Pork Belly
      2 Eggs
      Bok Choy
      Bamboo Shoots
      Green Onion
      Soy Sauce
      Nori
      Kamaboko (preferably the one with the swirls)
      Dried fish (Anchovy, Bonito, or Sardines)
      Dried Shiitake Mushroom
      Konbu
      Dark Soy Sauce
      Dry White Wine
      Sugar
      Mirin

Shoyu Tare

  1. Grind 1 oz of the dried fish of your choice and 1 large shiitake mushroom into powder using a food processor or blender.
  2. Next, place 2 1/2 cups of water into a large pot and place 2 oz of konbu. Once the water is simmering add the fish and mushroom powder. When it boils, remove from heat and strain.
  3. Measure out 6.5 oz of salt and 2 cups of dark soy sauce, and place into the strained liquid.
  4. Place in an airtight container and the tare can be kept in refrigerator for a few months

Pork Chashu

  1. Roll the pork belly, with the skin-side out and secure with cooking twine.
  2. Place pork belly on a hot skillet to brown all sides of the meat.
  3. Then place water, soy suace, white wine, and sugar into a large pot over heat to dissolve the sugar.
  4. Place the pork into the pot and bring to a boil, reducing the heat to medium and simmer for 25 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and let cool down before letting it soak in the sauce overnight

Menma

  1. In a pan put water, mirin, and the chashu sauce you made for the pork chashu.
  2. Bring to a boil then reduce the heat and let simmer for 25 minutes.Remove from heat and let cool.
  3. Store in a container with the liquid up to a week.

Seasoned Eggs

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil, slowly adding the eggs into the water for 7 minutes.
  2. Transfer the eggs into an ice bath for 3 minutes to chill, then peel.
  3. Mix the chashu seasoning liquid and mirin in a container, then place the eggs in.
  4. Refrigerate overnight. Store up to a few days.

Broth

  1. Buy bone broth, otherwise in a large pot boil the pork bone and fill the pot with water till it covers the bones.
  2. Bring the broth to a boil, reducing the heat to a simmer for 4 hours.
  3. Strain the broth into a container.

Assembly

  1. Get all the ingredients ready to go, boil hot water to cook the noodles and to heat up your serving bowl.
  2. Run the hot water over your bowl to heat up, then place the tare and soup into a pan to heat up before placing in the bowl.
  3. Cook the noodles in the boiling water and draining the noodles, shaking off as much of the excess water you can.Then place into the bowl with the tare and broth/
  4. Place 1 -2 pieces of chashu, a piece of nori, a slice of Kamaboko(aka naruto), 1 egg, and a couple pieces of menma.
  5. Serve.
